üî¨ Explaining the Physics

Air Density & Drag: A headwind increases the ball's speed relative to the air, dramatically increasing aerodynamic drag. Drag force scales with velocity squared, so a 20 mph headwind effectively adds to the ball's airspeed, creating much more resistance than calm air. The ball decelerates faster and falls short of its normal distance.
